What is Phishing?

Phishing is a form of cyber attack where fraudsters attempt to obtain sensitive information like passwords, credit card details, or personal identification information by impersonating legitimate sources. They typically use emails, websites, or text messages to deceive users into providing personal data.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Never click on suspicious links in emails or messages.
  • Always verify the source before providing sensitive information.
  • Check website URLs for security (e.g., HTTPS).
  • Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.
  • Regularly update passwords and use strong, unique passwords for each account.

Regulations: Ireland & Europe

GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

The GDPR is a regulation in EU law that focuses on data protection and privacy. It requires businesses to protect the personal data and privacy of EU citizens. In the case of phishing, organizations are required to notify users of potential breaches that could compromise their personal data.

Irish Cybersecurity Regulations

In Ireland, the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC) plays a critical role in managing cybersecurity issues, including phishing threats. The NCSC provides guidance on how individuals and organizations can protect themselves from online threats.

ePrivacy Directive

The ePrivacy Directive applies to electronic communications and includes provisions to prevent unsolicited communications such as phishing emails. It enforces strict regulations for businesses when processing data related to electronic communications.
